3.	Below are the list of indicators and variables used to calculate them for our weekly MO’s update regarding Fuel shock:
a.	Recruitment rate (RR): 
-	Definition: proportion of employers either currently recruiting or who had recruited in the previous month
-	How to calculate: dividing the number of employers who recruited or were attempting to recruit by the number of employers that responded to the survey question (excluding ‘unsure’ responses)
-	Business rules: excluding ‘unsure’ responses
-	Variable used: RecruitShort
-	Formula: RR =  (RecruitShort=Yes) / (RecruitShort=Yes + RecruitShort=No)
b.	Recruitment difficulty rate (RD):
-	Definition: proportion of recruiting employers who experienced difficulty hiring
-	How to calculate: the number of employers who recruited or are recruiting and reported difficulty, divided by all employers who recruited or are recruiting (excluding ‘unsure’ responses). 
-	Business rules: excluding ‘unsure’ responses
-	Variable used: Difficulty
-	Formula: RD = (Difficulty=Yes) / (Difficulty=Yes + Difficulty=No)
c.	Future staffing outlook (FSI – future staff increase)
-	Definition: proportion of employers expecting to increase staffing levels over the next 3 months
-	How to calculate: the number of employers who expect to increase staff over the next 3 months divided by all employers.
-	Business rules: includes ‘unsure’ responses in the denominator (the only indicator that does)
-	Variables used: FutStaffChange
-	Formula: FSI = (FutStaffChange = Increase) / FutStaffChange <>’’ (ie not blank)
d.	Costs as Greatest Concern (Cost_concern). 
-	Definition: proportion of employers whose greatest concern over the next 3 months is Costs (which includes the cost of fuel)
-	How to calculate: the number of employers whose greatest concern is Costs divided by all employers (excluding ‘unsure’ or blank responses).
-	Business rules: excluding both ‘unsure’ and blank responses from the denominator
-	Variables used: FutConcern
-	Formula: Cost_concern = (FutConcern = Costs) / FutConcern <>(Unsure or ’’)

Please note, for all calculation above, the sum of weight is used instead of the count for records. For example:
RR = [Sum(weight), filter (RecruitShort=Yes) ]/ [Sum(weight), filter (RecruitShort=Yes & RecruitShort=No)]
